Ferdinand Porsche

Ferdinand Porsche was an Austrian-German automotive engineer and the founder of Porsche. He established the company in 1931 in Stuttgart, Germany, originally as an engineering and automotive development firm.From an early age, Porsche showed a strong interest in mechanical engineering and vehicle technology. Before founding Porsche, he worked for major automotive companies including Daimler-Benz and Auto Union, where he helped design high-performance racing cars.One of his most famous achievements was designing the Volkswagen Beetle, which later became one of the most successful cars in automotive history. The vehicle became known for its reliability, simple design, and affordability.After World War II, his son Ferry Porsche continued the family legacy and built the first Porsche sports car, the Porsche 356, in 1948. This car established Porsche is reputation for building lightweight, high-performance sports cars. Ferdinand Porsche is philosophy focused on engineering excellence, speed, and driving performance, principles that continue to define the Porsche brand today.

Michael Leiters

Michael Leiters is a German automotive engineer and business executive known for his leadership in the luxury performance car industry. He has worked with several of the world is most prestigious automotive brands.Leiters spent more than a decade at Porsche, where he held important engineering leadership roles and contributed to the development of high-performance sports cars.In 2014, he joined Ferrari as Chief Technical Officer, where he helped lead the development of advanced performance technologies and future vehicle platforms.Later, he became CEO of McLaren Automotive, focusing on innovation, product development, and strengthening the company is position in the supercar market. Michael Leiters is expected to become the CEO of Porsche on 1 January 2026, bringing extensive engineering knowledge and leadership experience to guide the company into the future of performance and electrified vehicles.
